Bride’s story

I met Kyle in 2011 during my first internship in Cincinnati. I had a really good first impression of Kyle. I tried hanging out with Kyle that summer multiple times.. but he turned me down a lot (he’ll deny this to the very end). He did take me up on an offer to play tennis, which I’m sure he soon regretted :p. I asked him to see the last installment of the Harry Potter series, which he surprisingly said yes to. Before we knew it, it was time for me to leave Cincinnati and start my Junior year at UofM.

We only spoke a couple of times throughout the next school year and internship. At the end of our second internships, we both interviewed to join GE full-time. We both got offers which meant we’d be in training together for a month in 2013.

It was the first time we had seen each other in 2 years. I remember sitting in a restaurant in the lobby and seeing Kyle walk into the hotel with his red body bag slung over his shoulder (he has this backpack that can literally fit a person in it). He spotted us in the lobby and immediately walked over and grabbed the seat next to me. We very easily picked up where we left off and that month of training is filled with great memories with Kyle including searching for Greater’s Ice Cream in the middle of Detroit and convincing Kyle to watch the Bachelorette with me :). At the end of our training, Kyle went to Richmond while I went to Cincinnati for the next 12 months.

As soon as we parted ways, I knew I was going to miss Kyle a lot. I think Kyle felt the same way which is why we managed to see each other a handful of times during our first 6 months apart. In May 2015, Kyle surprised me by showing up to a GE event in Erie, PA. We officially started dating in good ol’ Erie and I know it’s one of the best decisions I’ve ever made.

Groom’s story

When I first met Neha in Cincinnati back in 2011 I thought that she was a very easy going and an affectionate person. You could tell that she could get along with anyone. She was one of those people that you wanted to get to know. Little did I know at the time, how well we would end up getting along :) .

We didn’t end up spending much time together that summer, the interns at GE thought they were better than us co-op folks that lived in Cincinnati. We did end up getting to play tennis, which I of course let Neha win, and seeing Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows. Even though those were the only two things we did together, they were common interests which we shared and enjoyed together as friends.

Fast forward through another summer of internships and school and you get to June of 2013. I had just arrived back from visiting my family in Germany and was ready to get started with real life. ITLP bootcamp was starting and I was looking forward to getting to know the people that I would be spending the next two years of my life with. Though I had established some great relationships throughout our month of training the relationship I was excited most about was the rekindled friendship I’d cultivated with Neha.

At the end of bootcamp I moved to Richmond to live with a Troll ( Josh) and Neha was moving to Cincinnati. Though we were in two different cities this did not keep us from video chatting almost every week and a booking trips to see each other (I’m sure there will be pictures and details of those elsewhere on this site). Going to see each other was fun but I could tell that Neha was starting to see me as more than a friend…or was that the other way around? IDK if that part is important...

After living in different cities for about a year there was a work trip that brought us together in Erie, PA. You're probably asking yourself what’s in Erie, PA? Before going there I would have asked the same question. There is a GE office, 50 bars, a casino, and the perfect place to ask the girl that you’ve been chasing for a year to start a relationship with you :D .

We ended up having to spend the next 4 months apart but that wasn’t so bad as we increased the frequency that we were traveling to see each other, and my family’s home was between Cincinnati and Richmond. Luckily the four months went by quickly and we were ready to travel to Budapest for a month on a work trip. Budapest was a city that we were both looking forward to visiting, though I think it was for different reasons. Neha was looking forward to traveling and I was trying to figure out the best place to propose. The answer to finding the best place to propose I found out was there isn’t really a right place so much as the right moment. This happened to be one night that we got to spend out at one of the only vegetarian restaurants in all of Hungary (seems that every places specialty is beef). We had a nice moonlit walk to and from the restaurants and everything came together and I don’t know if it was the wine or what but she said Yes.

After our european adventures we were back in the states and started our next adventure in CT. CT was full of making great memories with friends and my fiancé. It was the first time that I’d got to go to NYC and Neha showed me all the sites and took me to my first broadway show. There was a lot of first for us as a couple in CT and it will always hold a special place in my heart. However I think we both knew that we didn’t want to live in CT longterm and that led us to doing our fourth and final rotation of ITLP in Richmond, VA. This was one of the best decisions that we’ve made; as now we own our first home, we’ve adopted a four legged furball named Merlin, and have gotten closer with some old friends. We are both loving life here in VA and are looking forward to all of you sharing in our happiness at our wedding!!

"We started as colleagues, and slowly became friends. Through all the laughing and time together we ended up falling in love."

OUR LOVELINE

This is the story all about how… Wait wrong story. This is how we fell in Love.

2011
May 20

The day we met

Neha and I first met at GE in Cincinnati, OH. You’ll hear some discrepancies in why we didn’t spend more time together over this summer, but we had a good time and it was a lot of fun getting to know each other.

July 15

You're a Wizard Harry!

Neha and I had been texting back and forth about going to see Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows. We ended up both going with a group of friends to see the movie. It was so packed however that people ended up having to spread out to find seats. I got a text from Neha telling me that she had saved me a seat :). This should have been my first clue that she was a keeper.

2013
June 24

So We Meet Again

After not seeing each other for 2 years, Neha and I were reunited in the lobby of the Westin at the Detroit airport. We got the opportunity to spend the next month together through ITLP training. This month was full of making memories with each other and with new friends.

July 20

I'm Leavin' on a Jet Plane

Neha and I had finished our training in Michigan. It was time for us to move away from each other yet again. We sat in the Westin lobby and had breakfast together at 6am, one last time before catching our flights. It's funny thinking back that we had two very important meals in that lobby.

October 11

Cincinnati Visit

It had been a few months since the end of our ITLP training and I decided to go visit Neha in Cincinnati. It was October and I soon found out that Halloween is one of Neha’s favorite holidays. She had a fun weekend planned for us with a corn maze and a trip to a fruit farm that had some delicious apple cider.

November 15

Neha Visits the Capital of the South

Neha came to visit me in Richmond. She convinced me to take a segway tour of the city, we took a tour of Williamsburg (she downloaded a 'ghost' tour on accident and I played along), and she forced me to break into Jamestown after hours.

December 31

So long 2014...

After watching the Cincinnati bearcats bowl game in Charlotte,NC (which was dissapointing) I drove 10+ hours to visit Neha over New Years. This was a fun trip that included going downtown and watching the ball drop as well as learning to make eggplant parmesan.

2014
May 1

Taking a gamble...

I decided last minute to go the work event in Erie, PA. This turned out to be one of the best decisions I’ve made. I decided at this event to ask Neha to date me and after a little convincing she said Yes.

August 18

Popping the question!

Neha was in the Netherlands; I was in Germany. We met up in Budapest for yet another training. One evening I took Neha to a special resteraunt in the middle of the city, this was followed by a romantic walk back to our hotel where I proceeded to pop the question. If you're wondering I did get down on a knee.

August 31

Our Trip to Germany

This was one of my favorite parts about our work trip to Europe. We were able to travel to Munich and Neha was able to meet some of my family and got to see some of my favorite sites in Germany. There is never enough time during a vacation but the time we did get to spend here was well spent.

September 8

We move to Conneticut

Neha and I moved to Conneticut. We explored NYC, discovered a mutual love of broadway, and fell in love with the local creamery in Shelton, CT.

November 27

Turkey Day in Michigan

We celebrated our first Thanksgiving together in Michigan with Neha's family.

December 1

O Christmas Tree!

Getting our first christmas tree was an experience. We were trying to find a real tree but realized that we weren't going to be in Shelton for the majority of Christmas. We ended up getting a fake tree that was one of the most beautifully decorated trees that I've ever seen. The only thing I think that Neha wanted changed in our tree was the height...but our apartment was only so big, that explains why our new home's ceilings are so high :D .

December 25

HO HO HO!!

We celebrated our first Christmas in Proctorville, Ohio with my grandparents, sisters, parents, and Neha's folks! I think Neha loved her first real Christmas experience.

2015
March 23

Renting was so last year

Neha and I made the decision to purchase a home together. The decision was a hard one but we talked about it enough and decided that it was in our best interest to buy a home. This meant we were planning to stay in Richmond for the foreseeable future. I had lived in Richmond for a year at this point but Neha was taking a risk as she had not ever lived in the city, this was the first times that I felt like she was putting all her faith in me. That was a good feeling. Hopefully she doesn't regret it ;).

August 29

Here comes the Wizard

Neha and I have been going to the humane society frequently on the lookout for a dog to adopt. While we were looking for a dog, we occasionally hung out with a cat named Merlin at the shelter. We ended up taking him home and now he hangs out with us every day. Merlin is unlike any cat I've ever encountered. He loves to high five with his head, hang around with people, and sit on the couch (like actually sit). He has a few more "tricks" but you'll have to come visit to see those!!

"We are all a little weird and life's a little weird, and when we find someone who's weirdness is compatible with ours,we join up with them and fall in mutual weirdness and call it love."
